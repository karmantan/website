Kar Man Tan

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the mortality research group at the Federal Institute for Population Research (BiB). I completed my PhD in Economics in the Graduate School of Economics, Finance and Management (GSEFM) at Goethe University Frankfurt in April 2024. I am currently affiliated with the Einstein Center Population Diversity (ECPD) at Charité, International Center for Insurance Regulation (ICIR), and Frankfurt Laboratory for Experimental Economic Research (FLEX). My research interests include behavioral economics and applied microeconomics.


I enjoy watching anime in my free time. I am particularly fond of Gintama (銀魂) for its unapologetic humor, disrespectful indifference and character building. I also like Golden Kamuy (ゴールデンカムイ), another anime with ridiculous humor but a lot of historical references and factions representing different flavors of (morally ambiguous) ambition.
